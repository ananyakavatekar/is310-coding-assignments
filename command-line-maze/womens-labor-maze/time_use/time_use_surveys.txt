
ATUS stands for the American Time Use Survey that is conducted and published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

For much of modern history, unpaid household labor that women have held a significant portion of work in has not been included.

It was only starting in January of 2003 that the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics began including these activites.

Today it maintains that the:

“The American Time Use Survey measures the amount of time people spend doing various
activities, including unpaid household work.”
— U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics
